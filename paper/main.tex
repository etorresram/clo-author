\begin{abstract}
\noindent \singlespacing
The launch of ChatGPT in November 2022 delivered a common shock to labor markets worldwide, yet the only careful causal estimates come from OECD economies. This paper provides the first causal estimates of how large language model (LLM) exposure affected employment, hours, and wages in seven Latin American countries. I assemble harmonized labor force surveys for Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Ecuador, Mexico, Peru, and Uruguay (2021Q1--2025Q4), use the complementary-exposure score $\beta$ of \citet{Eloundou2023_gpt_exposure} as the treatment intensity, and estimate a continuous-treatment difference-in-differences specification country by country following \citet{CallawayCGBS2024_continuous}. The seven country-specific estimates are the headline result. Baseline (2021) formality is pre-registered as the central within-country heterogeneity margin. I contrast each country with the near-null Danish baseline of \citet{HumlumVestergaard2025_llm} and test four pre-committed narratives linking informality, adoption, and reallocation in each country separately.

\end{abstract}
